## 🤔 What are you good at?

<p>The assignment of tasks and responsibilities to the appropriate individuals is a longstanding challenge in building a company.</p><p>On a personal level, finding the proper role can also be problematic. It's essential to consider your own goals and strengths, as well as the current needs of the organization, in order to achieve its objectives and fulfill its mission.</p><p>One way to approach this challenge is to use the <strong>"Zone of Genius" framework</strong> outlined in The Big Leap by Gay Hendricks, which helps to ensure that roles and individuals are well-matched. This framework is simple and straightforward to use.</p>

## 🔶 4 “zones of function”

<!-- image: /images/cards/1c43aca295463362.png -->

<ol><li><strong>The incompetence zone.</strong> Refers to a situation where you need more relevant skills or understanding to complete tasks effectively.</li><li><strong>The competence zone. </strong>You can complete tasks proficiently, but you must stand out and bring something unique to the table. You meet the required standards.</li><li><strong>The excellence zone. </strong>You excel in your work and have likely developed your skills and expertise. You are highly skilled and stand out compared to others.</li><li><strong>The genius zone. </strong>The intersection of your greatest passion and your natural talents, where you can fully utilize and capitalize on your innate abilities.</li></ol><p><span style="background-color: rgb(252, 252, 252); color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">As you can imagine, highly successful people function in the lattermost zone.</span></p>

## 🔎 How to Find Your Genius Zone

<p>To determine your area of expertise, it's essential to<strong> consider your talents and passions</strong>. This involves examining your approach to tasks rather than just the work itself. Identifying what comes naturally to you can be challenging, especially if you have developed a wide range of skills. However, reflecting on these things can help you discover your "genius zone."</p><p><strong>4 Steps to Identify Zone of Genius:</strong></p><ol><li>Experiment &amp; Collect</li><li>Build Your Matrix</li><li>Identify Your Zones</li><li>Execute</li></ol>

## 🔬 1. Experiment & Collect

<p>Contrary to popular belief, it is optional to have a clear career path mapped out upon graduating college.</p><p>Instead,<strong> it is important to explore different options</strong> and gather information to make informed decisions. This can be done through experimentation and trying different working styles without committing to a specific job or career.</p><p>The goal is to <strong>gain diverse experience</strong>s that will allow you to assess your skills and passions. It is also helpful to consider both your perspective and the perspectives of others when collecting data.</p><p>You can ask others about their experiences working with you, including their observations on your strengths, weaknesses, and times when they have seen you perform at your best.</p><ul><li>What are you great at?</li><li>What are you bad at?</li><li>When do they perceive you as in flow?</li></ul>

## ⚒️ 2. Build Your Matrix

<!-- image: /images/cards/de1c06a249160916.png -->

<p>The next step is to use the data collected from your experimentation to create a "skill map." This visual represents your competency level in various skills, with a scale ranging from 0 (low competency) to 1 (high competency).</p><p>To fully understand your strengths and areas for growth, it is essential to consider your passion for each skill. One way to do this is to build a matrix with competency on one axis and passion on the other, allowing you to see where your most significant potential lies.</p>

## ⚗️ 3. Identify Your Zones

<!-- image: /images/cards/d1bda95089f83cb6.png -->

<p><strong>The four key zones to identify on your matrix:</strong></p><p><strong>1. Zone of Incompetence</strong>: you are bad at these things; outsource to others who are good at them.</p><p><strong>2. Zone of Competence</strong>: you are ok at these things; outsource to others who are as good or better at them.</p><p><strong>3. Zone of Excellence:</strong> you are excellent at these things, but you don’t love them</p><p>(This is the "danger zone”: you will be asked to work here given your competency, but it can be a trap)</p><p><strong>4. Zone of Genius</strong>: you are excellent at these things, and you love to do them</p>

## 🏃 4. Execute

<p>Once you have identified your "Zone of Genius," you must<strong> make the most of this area of strength</strong>. This may involve spending more time on tasks and activities that align with your passions and natural talents while minimizing time spent on tasks that do not align with your Zone of Genius.</p><p>If you work in a company, having a candid conversation with your team and managers about your strengths and areas of focus can be helpful. <strong>Encouraging the entire team to go through this process can help align roles and responsibilities better. </strong></p><p>If you can't fully use your Zone of Genius in your current role or company, you should&nbsp;consider changing it to find a better fit.</p><p>If you are self-employed, you must be honest about which daily activities align with your Zone of Genius and consider outsourcing tasks that do not fall within this area.</p><p><strong>Maximizing your time in your Zone of Genius can improve your results and performance.</strong></p>

## 💪 Pushing boundaries

<p>Establishing a culture that promotes and supports the development of each individual's "genius zone" can be challenging and may require stepping out of one's comfort zone and pushing personal boundaries in order to grow. However, if done effectively, this process can lead to increased resilience within the business and a greater level of authenticity that sets your company apart from competitors and attracts clients.</p>
