## 😤 Source of conflicts

<p>One of the most common sources of conflict and dissatisfaction in teams stems from a lack of role clarity between team members and between the team and the leader.</p><p>To resolve such difficulties in teamwork, a workshop can be held to clarify each role's areas of responsibility.</p><p>Let's see how to organize one.</p>

## 👨‍💻 1. Define the roles in your team (5 minutes)

<!-- image: /images/cards/d1580df101a74e0d.png -->

<p>Draw a table on the board with enough space to list the different roles on the team. Leave room for "unassigned responsibilities"; this part comes in handy later.</p><p>Ask each participant what their role on the team is, and write the answers on post-its.</p><p><em>Some roles can be combined; for example, if you have Front-End and Back-End developers, you can just list them as "Developer."</em></p>

## 🔧 2. Define your responsibilities (10 minutes)

<p>Each team member writes down 3-5 key things on post-its that they feel they are responsible for in their role. Then ask them to sort them in order of priority.</p><p>When everything is ready, set the post-its aside.</p>

## 👩‍🎨 3. Identify the responsibilities of the other roles (10 minutes)

<p>Now ask each team member to write down what they think are the top 1-2 priorities of each of the other roles on the team.</p><p><em>If any responsibilities are difficult to assign to a role, they can be put on a separate pile. They will come in handy for further discussion.</em></p>

## 👥 4. Discuss each role (30 minutes)

<!-- image: /images/cards/ae4ad2580a8fe6f3.png -->

<ol><li>Select one role, for example, <strong>Product Manager</strong>.</li><li>Ask the representative(s) of that role to read what they wrote down as their top 3-5 priorities and place them in the appropriate grid square.</li><li>Then, ask each team member to read what they wrote down as the Sales Manager's top 1-2 priorities.</li><li>At this point, the Sales Manager can <strong>accept or reject</strong> responsibilities that other team members have suggested.</li><li>If a role representative declines, the team should figure out which role should be assigned that responsibility or&nbsp;move it to the "Not Assigned" column if it does not currently have an owner<strong>.</strong></li></ol><p><strong>Repeat this exercise</strong> for every role in the team and discuss the unassigned responsibilities as you go to see if they fit anywhere.</p>

## 👣 5. Identify the next steps (5 minutes)

<p>At the end of the exercise, it will be clear if the team is aligned or if any unallocated responsibilities require owners.</p><p>The following steps may involve identifying who owns them. You may need to bring in an outsider to help resolve problems.</p><p>Trust someone to record the results and share them with the team.</p>

## 👉 What's next?

<p>The workshop's primary goal is to develop a team understanding of roles and responsibilities. Equally important is to put the agreements into practice, which should be given ample attention as we continue to work together.</p><p>It's also helpful to <strong>review roles and responsibilities</strong> as you add new roles or new people come in.</p>
