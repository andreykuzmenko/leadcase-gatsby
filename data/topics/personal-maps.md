## 🤝 Team relations

<p>How well do you know your colleagues? How easy is it for your teammates to communicate with each other? What about trust within the team?</p><p>If you have difficulty answering any of the questions, then it might be helpful to build <strong>personal maps</strong> of each other.</p>

## 🗺 Personal map

<!-- image: /images/cards/482423acd923bc15.jpeg -->

<p><strong>Personal Map</strong> is a practice from Management 3.0 designed to improve team interaction. It's a great way to find out what motivates everyone involved. It also allows people to develop empathy and meaningful connections with each other.</p><p>Knowing more about each other helps people <strong>trust their colleagues</strong> more, strengthening the team.</p>

## 📔 How to use?

<ol><li>Before starting the exercise, <strong>define a list of categories. </strong>Choose categories according to the type of group — for example, home, education, work, hobbies, family, friends, goals, and values.</li><li>Give each person a blank sheet of paper.</li><li>Write the person's name in the middle of the sheet and build a map around it.</li></ol>

## 💡 Ideas for the format

<ul><li><strong>Each person makes a map of himself</strong>. Subsequently, people exchange their cards and present them. Then the team guesses whose card it is.</li><li><strong>Creating cards in pairs. </strong>People pair up and make each other's cards in an interview format.</li><li><strong>Write the person's name and make a card about them during the week.</strong> During a coffee or lunch break, chat with a colleague asking questions to complete the card. But be careful not to simplify the task by asking questions like "What are your hobbies?"</li><li><strong>"Test" with personal cards. </strong>After you've done everyone's cards in the team, try a little fun test. Ask each other questions related to the cards and reward each correct answer with some reward.</li><li><strong>Online Map</strong>. If you have a team that spends a limited amount of time together, you can do personal map exercises remotely, for example, with a video call and an online board like Miro.</li></ul>

## 👌 Recommendations

<ol><li>When discussing maps, it is better to have <strong>each team member present someone else's map</strong>. This approach naturally provokes questions, discussions, moments of connection and understanding between people on your team.</li><li>In the interview format, you can allow <strong>10 minutes each way</strong> and another three minutes for introducing each other's cards.</li></ol><p>Be aware that personal cards are not a one-time exercise; they develop as life goes on!</p>
