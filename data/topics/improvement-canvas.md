## 🤯 Solving problems

<p>An inseparable part of the development and growth of any organization or team is the presence of complexities and problems that they have to face. Very often, <span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">there are no simple and quick solutions for resolving the issues. As a result, it may take a lot of time and experiments to achieve the desired result.</span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">An excellent tool that can be useful for systematic problem-solving is</span> <strong style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);"> </strong><strong>Improvement Canvas</strong>.</p>

## 🔥 Improvement Canvas

<!-- image: /images/cards/135c704a84cfeaa.png -->

<p><strong>Improvement Canvas </strong>is a template (canvas) that allows you to plan changes to the desired state through small steps.</p><p>The canvas <span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">consists of five parts:</span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">1. </span>Title of the Topic</p><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">2. </span>Current State</p><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">3. </span>"Ideal" state (Definition of awesome)</p><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">4. </span>Next State<span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);"> </span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">5. First Steps</span></p>

## 🔖 Title of the Topic

<p>Filling the canvas begins with formulating the problem/challenge you want to solve.</p><p>The topic can be known before the start of the meeting, or it can be chosen as a result of brainstorming.</p><p><strong><em>Tip</em></strong><em>: if you have trouble clearly articulating the topic, start by describing the current state and then return to defining the topic.</em></p>

## 🔥 Current state

<p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">To get support from all stakeholders, </span><strong style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">it is necessary to have a common understanding of the current situation and problem. </strong></p><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">Give everyone two minutes to record their opinion on the current situation/problem on sticky notes. Then each person presents and explains their notes. The sticky notes are placed next to the canvas. After all the notes are shown, the team is given a task to summarize the “Problem/Current State” in a few sentences or points. </span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">This two-step process is important as it forms a shared understanding and reveals different perspectives on the situation.</span></p>

## 💎 "Ideal" state (Definition of awesome)

<p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">Ask the participants what should be the "ideal" state. If it was already implemented, what would it look and feel like?</span></p><p><strong style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">"Ideal state" can be a distant vision</strong><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">, possibly even unrealistic over the next six months/year. The idea is to create a shared vision of the goal to be strived for. </span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">Ask the team to describe this vision in a couple of points (maximum 5) or a couple of sentences (top 3). </span></p>

## 🤔 Next state

<p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">Identify the first realistic sub-goal to your vision - the next targeted state.</span></p><p><strong style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">Choose a period (date) and create a checklist</strong><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);"> of three to four things you want to accomplish by then. </span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">For example: </span><em style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">"In 6 weeks, we have achieved the following:..."</em><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);"> followed by a list of statements describing what has changed. The criteria should be clear and easily measurable. </span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">For example: </span><em style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">"Within a week, we have no customer complaints."</em></p>

## 🤔 First steps

<p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">You can start by brainstorming potential small actions the team could take to get the desired end state.</span></p><p><strong style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">If it's a large task, ask: where would we start? </strong><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">Turn that into action. Do not discard proposals, and don’t censor stupid ideas right now.</span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">The actions don’t require a complete plan to reach the end goal. </span><strong style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">Define a couple of small steps that</strong><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);"> can be taken to get started. Once the set of possible actions is defined, select (via a vote or discussion) which three will become our First Steps. These three ‘stickers’ are placed inside three red squares, Action Fillers, for tackling tasks. Finally, assign responsible for the actions.</span></p>

## 🔍 Tracking progress

<p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">At the end of the meeting, </span><strong style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">agree on the regularity and format of tracking your progress toward the goal</strong><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">. Depending on the topic and its importance, the progress can be tracked daily, several times a week, or weekly. </span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">In any case, it is essential not to forget the agreements and periodically analyze the progress.</span></p>

## 🎯 Evaluation of results

<p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">Once the "Next Desired State" deadline has passed, it's time to analyze the results.</span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">To do this, ask the team:</span></p><ul><li><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">Have we achieved the desired state?</span></li><li><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">How far are we from the Current State? </span></li><li><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">How close did we get to our definition of “ideal”?</span></li><li><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">Is our topic still relevant? </span></li></ul><p><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">If yes, </span><strong style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">define the new next desired state</strong><span style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51);">. If not, create a new </span>Improvement Canvas.</p>
